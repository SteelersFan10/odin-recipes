Kielbasa & Pierogies:

A Polish-American Staple

Pan seared Kielbasa and Pierogies with butter and onions

This is a classic polish dish known to satisfy even the hungriest of bellies. Pierogies are essentially pasta dumplings filled with potatoes, cheese, sauerkraut, a combination of the three, or other creative fillings.

For example, click here for an excellent buffalo chicken style pierogie recipe.

Ingredients

  • 25 frozen pierogies (Mrs. T's or local polish deli)
  • 1 lb Kielbasa or smoked sausage (any flavor)
  • 5 tbs butter (what would we do without it?)
  • 1 yellow onion (or sweet onion)

Instructions

  1. Add 2 tbsp of butter to large skillet over medium heat
  2. Add defrosted pierogies and saute until golden brown
  3. Remove pierogies, set aside and cover
  4. Add 1 more tbsp of butter, and chopped kielbasa into pan. Cook until browned.
  5. Add 2 tbsp butter, plus chopped onion. Saute until texture is nice and soft.
  6. Add pierogies back in, cook on low while stirring periodically. Serve hot!
